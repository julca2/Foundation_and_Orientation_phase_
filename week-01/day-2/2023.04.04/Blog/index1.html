What is dragon?

The dragon is a winged, serpentine, fire-breathing creature with a barbed tail. The dragon emerges in Ancient Greece and usually represented a large serpent. Across the world's cultures, the dragon is a famous mythical creature, historically representing fierceness and evil in Christian stories. Dragon imagery has been used throughout history by warriors such as the Norse invaders, who had painted dragons on their shields. However, in Asia, the dragon remains to be a prestigious creature and once featured on the Chinese flag. The dragon also features prominently on the flag of Wales.

What is unicorn?

The unicorn looks like a white horse but has a single horn on its forehead. The unicorn features in Ancient Greek mythology and Medieval texts whilst also appearing in the ancient myths of China and India. The unicorn is majestic and pure whilst commonly representing strength and fierceness.
